The number

His shirt on the Lakers was number 8, but from the 2006-07 season it became 24. Some say because of Michael Jordan's number 23, others say it was just a "turn in time" of the star, who had already used this numbering before in High School. But the strongest rumor is that Nike, its supplier of sporting goods would have asked him to change the number, since Adidas, which supplied the equipment for Bryant previously, still has tennis lines that use the brand Bryant number 8 of the player.

The film and the Oscar

The legendary former Los Angeles Lakers player won an Oscar for Best Animated Short Film in 2018 for the Dear Basketball production. The animation was based on a poem that Kobe wrote and published when he decided to quit basketball.

The Death

At approximately 10 a.m. on the west coast of the United States on January 26, 2020, Bryant and eight other people died in a helicopter crash outside Calabasas, in Southern California. His thirteen year old daughter, Gianna, was on the aircraft with him, along with the her teammate and one of her friend's parents, and the pilot. They were on their way to a game of basketball. According to Los Angeles County officials, there were no survivors of the accident. Preliminary reports stated that the Sikorsky S-76 helicopter would have crashed into a mountainous region about the city of Calabasas, during a dense fog. According to witnesses, the helicopter was in flames before the fall. Local authorities are still investigating the incident.
